In Kerala, the marriage ceremonies may include an elaborate ritual Kettu Kalyanam, followed by the Sambandham, which means a contract between bride and groom. During the Sambandham ceremony, the groom, with permission from elders, gives the bride a wedding shawl or pudava. Upon receiving the cloth, she hands over the thamboola (betel leaves and arecanut) to the groom.
